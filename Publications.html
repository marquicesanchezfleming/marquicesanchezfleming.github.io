  1. ZTF Observations of the Fast Blue Optical Transient AT2024lhs
    Jada L. Vail, Jason Sevilla (Cornell), Harlan T. Phillips (UC Berkeley), Jack T. Pope, Marquice Sanchez-Fleming, Anna Y. Q. Ho (Cornell), Michael W. Coughlin (UMN), Igor Andreoni (UMD), Nabeel Rehemtulla (Northwestern), June 2024
    We report ZTF observations of a blue rapidly evolving transient, AT2024lhs (ZTF24aascytf). The host galaxy has a Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) redshift of z = 0.038, implying a peak absolute magnitude of M_g = -17.5 mag.

  2. Photometry and Spectroscopy of the Fast Optical Transient AT2024kmq
    Jason Sevilla, Jada L. Vail (Cornell), Chang Liu (Northwestern), Gokul Srinivasaragavan (UMD), Daniel Perley (LJMU), Harlan Phillips (Berkeley), Jack Pope, Marquice Sanchez-Fleming, Anna Y. Q. Ho (Cornell), June 2024
    We report photometric and spectroscopic observations of a rapidly evolving transient, AT2024kmq (ZTF24aapvieu). A Gemini spectrum shows that the host-galaxy redshift is z = 0.192 and that the galaxy has no active star formation, unlike the hosts of most other rapidly evolving transients discovered to date.
